The Algorithm Scrolls On

Liam scrolls through Instagram.

The glow of his phone paints his face in cold blue light.

Each post promises a better home, a better life—perfectly staged rooms, smiling strangers in curated spaces.

The Algorithm whispers with every swipe: This could be yours.

AD: "Brighten your evenings with modern simplicity". Liam presses Buy Now.

Two days later, it stands in his living room - white, sleek and silent. Its glow feels less like warmth and more like surveillance.

The algorithm scrolls on. AD: "Handcrafted wood, authentic charm". The table arrives, heavy and sharp, with a foreign scent.

It looks very natural, but not for Liam.

The algorithm scrolls on. AD: "Luxury comfort, now 20% off."

When the sofa arrives, it consumes the room. Plush, rich, inviting. But he feels like a guest in his own house.

The algorithm scrolls on. AD: "Low maintenance. Maximum style". Little glass orbs sit on his windowsill, arranged like props on a set.

The algorithm scrolls on. AD: "Bold colours. Make a statement." Hung on his wall, it stares back at him. Demanding attention, drowning him out.

The algorithm scrolls on. AD: "Clean lines. Endless space for books you'll never read”. He assembles it late at night. When it stands tall, the room feels emptier, colder. Like it belongs to someone else.

The algorithm scrolls on. AD: "Timeless design. Transform your space". The rug unfurls, swallowing the floor.

Each step feels unknown. As though he's walking in someone else's shoes.

The algorithm scrolls on. AD: "Your home, but smarter." It arrives quickly, waiting for his voice. The house answers when he speaks. But he doesn't feel heard.

The algorithm scrolls on. The house is full now. Every corner, every shelf, every wall is dressed in someone else's choices.

Liam slumps into his velvet sofa. The rug beneath his feet feels cold. The lamp hums faintly. The bookshelf towers. The smart speaker blinks, listening.

The room looks perfect. It looks nothing like the place that Liam once called home.

And in that moment, he realises,

He is not who he was when he first opened the app.

The algorithm has not only furnished his house, it has furnished his identity.

But the phone still glows in his hand. Another post waits, eager for his thumb.

The screen goes black. For the first time, the house is silent.

Liam looks around, uneasy but free.

He tries to remember what he wanted before the feed told him who to be.

The algorithm waits in the dark, patient, certain he will return.

Liam’s house was never really his. Each item was chosen for him, not by him.

That’s what algorithms do: they don’t just shape your feed, they shape you.

The real danger isn’t buying things you don’t need,

It’s becoming someone you never chose to be.

So ask yourself: will you keep scrolling? Or will you put the phone down?
